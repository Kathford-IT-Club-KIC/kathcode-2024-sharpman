Key Points: Top 10 Most Affected Districts by Forest Fires in Nepal

  • Surkhet has the highest number of forest fires among the top 10 affected districts in Nepal, with approximately 350 incidents.
  • Salyan is the second most affected district, with just under 300 forest fires.
  • Dang, Kailali, and Chitwan each experience around 250 forest fires, indicating significant fire activity in these districts.
  • The districts of Bardia, Doti, Dadeldhura, Parsa, and Ieft show a gradual decrease in the number of forest fires, with each district having fewer than 200 incidents, indicating a lower but still considerable impact.

Forest Fire Incidents in Nepal

Steady Low Incidents: From June 2023 to January 2024, the number of forest fires remains relatively low and stable, with monthly incidents under 500.

Gradual Increase: There is a slight increase in forest fire incidents starting from February 2024.

Sharp Peak: A dramatic spike occurs in March 2024, with the number of forest fires surging to over 3500. This indicates a significant outbreak of forest fires during this month.

Rapid Decline: Following the peak in March, the number of forest fires sharply declines in April 2024.

Decrease Towards Baseline: By May and June 2024, the number of forest fires continues to drop, nearing the baseline levels observed in the earlier part of the period.
